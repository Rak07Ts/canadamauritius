Key Meetings in Port-Louis: MCCI and Corporate Leadership Center

On Tuesday, January 13, 2026, our President Padminee Chundunsing and Strategic Advisor Daniel Paquette had the privilege of meeting Dr. Drishtysingh Ramdenee, Secretary General of the Mauritius Chamber of Commerce and Industry (MCCI), at the Anglo Mauritius House in Port-Louis.

It was a fruitful and substantive discussion, focused on collaboration and the concrete opportunities available to leverage the Mauritian diaspora as a driver of sustainable economic development — both for Mauritius and for Canada.

Mauritius Chamber of Commerce and Industry

The MCCI is one of Mauritius's leading private sector institutions, representing a broad spectrum of businesses and sectors. Its Secretary General, Dr. Ramdenee, brings deep institutional knowledge of the Mauritian business environment and a long-standing commitment to strengthening bilateral and multilateral trade relationships.

The conversation explored the complementary strengths of Canada and Mauritius — and identified how the diaspora network, in particular, could serve as an active, concrete bridge between the two economies. The diaspora is not merely a symbolic asset; it is a living network of professionals, entrepreneurs and institutional actors with the relationships, cultural fluency and business insight to accelerate bilateral engagement.

Meeting with the Corporate Leadership Center

Later that day, Padminee Chundunsing and Daniel Paquette also met with Amaresh Ramlugan, Founder-Director of The Corporate Leadership Center Ltd. (CLCL), at Ebene Cybercity, Mauritius.

CLCL is a recognized leader in executive development and organizational transformation in Mauritius. The meeting offered an opportunity to explore potential collaboration in the areas of leadership capacity-building, professional development programs, and the creation of pathways that connect Canadian expertise with Mauritian institutions.

A Day of Tangible Progress

These two meetings reflected the Venture's approach to bilateral engagement: targeted, structured conversations with credible, senior counterparts — conversations designed not to explore possibilities in the abstract, but to identify concrete next steps.

January 13 was one of several productive days in a mission that affirmed the readiness of Mauritian institutions to engage seriously and substantively with Canadian partners.

For more information on the Venture's mission activities and partnership opportunities, contact info@canadamauritius.com.

Mauritius as a Gateway to Africa

For Canadian businesses and investors looking to expand into Africa and the Indian Ocean region, Mauritius offers a uniquely accessible, stable and well-connected entry point — one that is often underestimated and consistently underused.

Why Mauritius

Mauritius consistently ranks among the top business environments in Africa. The World Bank's Ease of Doing Business index, the Mo Ibrahim Index of African Governance and Transparency International's Corruption Perceptions Index all place Mauritius at or near the top of the continent — year after year.

What makes Mauritius distinctive as a business location is the combination of attributes it offers:

  • A competitive and internationally recognized tax and investment framework
  • A legal system rooted in both English common law and French civil law — uniquely suited to structuring deals across Anglophone and Francophone Africa
  • A skilled, bilingual workforce with strong professional services capability
  • A deep network of tax treaties and investment protection agreements covering most of Africa, Asia and Europe
  • World-class financial services infrastructure including banking, fund administration, trusts and insurance
  • A stable democracy with consistent rule of law and an independent judiciary

Canada-Africa Through a Mauritian Lens

Canada had been deepening its commitment to the African continent through trade policy, development finance and bilateral engagement. Mauritius offered a way to accelerate and de-risk that engagement — using an established, trusted jurisdiction as a foundation for structuring, managing and executing investment across a continent that rewards preparedness.

The Canada-Mauritius bilateral relationship, underpinned by Commonwealth ties, democratic values and a shared commitment to international rules-based frameworks, provided a natural and credible foundation for this kind of strategic partnership. What it had lacked — until now — was a dedicated vehicle to make that partnership work in practice.

"Mauritius is not just a destination. It is a platform — a place from which you can reach an entire continent with the legal, financial and human infrastructure to do it properly."

— Canada Mauritius Trade and Investment Venture

The Diaspora Advantage

The Mauritian diaspora in Canada is one of the most valuable — and most underutilized — assets in the bilateral relationship. These individuals combine Canadian market knowledge, professional networks and Mauritian cultural and institutional understanding in a way that no formal mechanism can easily replicate.

They are founders, executives, investors, academics and community leaders. Many maintain active connections to Mauritius and to broader African networks. They understand both operating environments on a personal level. They represent, in aggregate, a remarkable bridge — one the Venture is designed to activate.

The Venture worked to connect diaspora leaders with institutions, investors and businesses in ways that accelerated the bilateral relationship — turning a latent asset into an active one.

A Strategic Moment

The timing for Canada-Mauritius engagement was right. Mauritius was actively diversifying its international partnerships and looking for new high-quality economic relationships. Canada was increasingly looking to the Indian Ocean region as a strategic priority, with growing interest from both the private sector and government in expanding beyond traditional markets.

The Venture's role was to ensure that this window of opportunity was seized — with the structure, the networks and the visibility to make the connection durable and commercially meaningful. The exploratory business mission of January 2026 was a first step in that direction.

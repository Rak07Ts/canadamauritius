January 15, 2026 — Mauritius

Final Day of Mission: Meeting with the Minister of Foreign Affairs

Thursday, January 15, 2026, marked the final day of our exploratory business mission to Mauritius — and it concluded on a particularly inspiring and significant note, reflecting the depth of engagement the Venture has built in a remarkably short time.

The mission, led by our President Padminee Chundunsing and Strategic Advisor Daniel Paquette, had been a series of substantive, high-level meetings across government and the private sector. Its final day brought two conversations that underscored both the reach and the momentum of Canada-Mauritius bilateral engagement.

Meeting with the Hon. Dhananjay Ramful

We had the honour of meeting The Hon. Dhananjay RAMFUL, Minister of Foreign Affairs, Regional Integration and International Trade of Mauritius. The discussion was meaningful, forward-looking and substantive — reflecting a strong willingness on both sides to strengthen bilateral ties.

Key areas of discussion included:

  • Deepening cooperation in the tourism sector, an area of significant complementary potential between Canada and Mauritius
  • Economic development priorities and how Canadian expertise and capital can align with Mauritius's growth agenda
  • Cultural exchange as a foundation for durable people-to-people ties
  • Investment pathways and the creation of sustainable partnerships that benefit communities in both countries

The conversation reflected the shared conviction that the Canada-Mauritius relationship has significant untapped potential — and that both sides are ready to invest in making it work at a higher level.

Meeting with Mr. Brian AhChuen, Honorary Consul of Canada

The mission also included a valuable meeting with Mr. Brian AhChuen, Honorary Consul of Canada in Mauritius. This conversation enriched the Venture's understanding of the diplomatic landscape and opened further avenues for collaboration, bilateral relations, and the identification of future opportunities.

The Honorary Consul's perspective provided important context on the institutional environment and the practical dimensions of Canada-Mauritius engagement — insights that will inform the Venture's planning for future missions and bilateral initiatives.

Leaving Energized

We left this mission energized by the openness, professionalism and shared vision expressed throughout our meetings. From the Mauritius Chamber of Commerce and Industry to the Economic Development Board, from private sector leaders to the highest levels of government, the message was consistent: Mauritius is ready to engage, and the relationship with Canada holds enormous promise.

The Canada Mauritius Trade and Investment Venture looks forward to building on this positive momentum in the months ahead — translating the relationships and conversations of this mission into concrete, durable bilateral outcomes.

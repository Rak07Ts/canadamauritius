Young Leaders Program

Empowering Youth. Strengthening Canada–Africa Connections.

Overview

Investing in the Next Generation of Leaders

The Young Leaders Program is a youth leadership and mentorship initiative delivered to strengthen people-to-people connections between Canada and Africa, including Mauritius. The program equips young people in Canada with leadership skills, cross-cultural understanding, and opportunities for civic and community engagement — recognizing youth as key contributors to inclusive and sustainable international relationships.

We believe that strong international and multicultural relationships begin at the community level. By mentoring and empowering youth in Canada — particularly those from African, diaspora, and equity-deserving communities — we foster inclusive leadership and promote long-term social cohesion and global engagement.

Who the Program Is For
  • Youth and young adults in Canada (ages 18–35)
  • Students, newcomers, young professionals and emerging leaders
  • Youth from African and Afro-descendant communities
  • Allies interested in global engagement and multicultural connection
Program Objectives

What We Aim to Achieve

01

Leadership Capacity

Develop youth leadership capacity through mentorship and skills training, building the confidence and competence needed for civic and professional life.

02

Multiculturalism & Inclusion

Promote multiculturalism and social inclusion through education, dialogue and culturally responsive programming that reflects Canada's diversity.

03

Civic Engagement

Support civic and community engagement among young people in Canada, encouraging active participation in community life and democratic institutions.

04

People-to-People Ties

Strengthen Canada–Africa connections by reducing barriers to participation for equity-deserving and underrepresented youth.

What We Offer

Program Components

Mentorship & Leadership Development

Participants are paired with experienced mentors from community organizations, public service and the non-profit sector. The program builds leadership, communication, teamwork and confidence.

Multicultural & Global Learning

Through workshops and dialogue, participants explore multiculturalism in Canada, Africa–Canada connections and global citizenship — strengthening awareness and mutual understanding.

Community Engagement

Participants engage in community initiatives, cultural events and volunteer activities that promote inclusion, social cohesion and active citizenship.

Youth-Led Initiatives

Participants design and implement small-scale projects that address community needs, foster dialogue and apply leadership skills in real-world settings.

Impact

What Participants Gain

Through the Young Leaders Program, participants gain:

  • Leadership and employability skills
  • Strong mentorship and peer networks
  • Greater confidence and civic engagement
  • A deeper understanding of multiculturalism and international connections

Communities benefit from increased youth participation, stronger social cohesion and sustainable people-to-people ties between Canada and Africa.

Our Commitment to Inclusion

The program is grounded in equity, diversity and inclusion — ensuring accessible participation, inclusive outreach and culturally responsive programming for all participants.

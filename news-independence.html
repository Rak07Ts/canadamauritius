Celebrating Mauritian Independence with the Diaspora Community

On March 14, 2026, members of the Canada Mauritius Trade and Investment Venture joined the Mauritian diaspora community in Canada to celebrate Mauritian Independence — a joyful, proud and meaningful occasion that brought together community members of all backgrounds under the colours of the Mauritian flag.

Mauritius gained independence on March 12, 1968, and every year, Mauritians at home and abroad mark this milestone with pride and reflection. This year's celebration in Canada was a testament to the vitality of the diaspora community — and to the deep sense of identity and belonging that Mauritians carry with them wherever they go.

Pride, Heritage and Partnership

For the Canada Mauritius Trade and Investment Venture, this celebration is also a reminder of the values that anchor the bilateral relationship: a shared commitment to democratic governance, inclusive growth, and the belief that people-to-people ties are the foundation of lasting economic partnerships.

The Mauritian diaspora in Canada — active, entrepreneurial and deeply connected to both countries — is central to the Venture's work. These are the individuals who bridge cultures, translate contexts, and turn goodwill into real economic opportunity.

Looking Ahead

As Mauritius continues to grow as a strategic partner and gateway to Africa, and as Canada deepens its engagement with the African continent, moments like this independence celebration carry increasing significance. They remind us that the foundation of a strong bilateral relationship is built not only in boardrooms and government offices, but in communities — at tables, celebrations and conversations that honour who we are and where we come from.

The Venture remains committed to supporting and engaging the Mauritian diaspora across Canada as a core pillar of its bilateral mission. To get involved, write to info@canadamauritius.com.
